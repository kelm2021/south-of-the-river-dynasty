1.2 Trading

Trading of players and draft picks (up to 3 drafts away) are allowed. Trades will process immediately and will be reversed if vetoed or under investigation for collusion. Tradable assets include players, draft picks, and FAAB $.

1.2.1 Trade Collusion

If any owners are suspected of accepting or offering bribes to enhance their or another owner's team via trade, trading any assets other than fantasy players, draft picks, or FAAB $, or otherwise found guilty of engaging in any form of collusion*, all parties involved in the trade will be subject to punishment:

  • First Offense – Immediate reduction of FAAB budget by 80%
  • Second Offense – Immediate freeze on all transaction types for the next 18 weeks of regular season and playoff games, including trades and waiver wire bids
  • Third Offense – A league meeting of all members not involved in any of the improper trades/activity will take place to discuss next steps

All trades found guilty of collusion will be reversed/denied. If the trade affected the outcome of previous games, those results will be revised.

*Collusion includes the organizing of veto votes against an acceptable trade.

The commissioner will err on the side of letting owners manage their teams as they see fit. Any collusion will need to be clear and obvious. The commissioner reserves the right to at the very least assess the thinking of any team involved in a heavily lopsided trade.

1.2.2 Lending Players Prohibition

Any player traded from a team may not be re-acquired via trade within 6 weeks of the original trade. Any teams involved in this behavior will be subject to the aforementioned collusion punishments.

1.2.3 Trade Deadline

The trade deadline will be set for the Saturday of the Week 11 games of the NFL season.

1.2.4 Trade Restrictions

You may not trade draft picks that are more than 3 drafts away. For example, during the 2021 season you cannot trade any picks in or after the 2025 draft. You may only trade FAAB $ from the current season.

1.2.5 Veto / Trade Process

A total of 7 veto votes are required to veto a trade. Vetoes are only appropriate in instances of collusion or an egregious mismatch of value (i.e. CMC for a backup kicker). Not liking a trade does not warrant a veto.

1.3 Waiver Wire

FAAB waiver wire system. Each team begins the season with $100. $0 bids are allowed. Bids process Thursdays and Sundays at 9 AM EST. FAAB budgets reset each season in January.

Section 2: Drafting

2.1 Draft Order

The draft order each year will be a linear draft in reverse order of the previous year's regular season standings.

2.2 Rookie Drafts

Rookie drafts consist of 3 rounds of drafting, with 5 minutes allotted for each pick. To make room for incoming players, benches will be expanded by 5 spots. These spots will be removed in mid-August, at which point you will need to have cut the necessary players.

Any spots not filled during the draft can be filled immediately afterward from the waiver wire.

2.3 Draft Day Trades

If a trade is being made during the draft, one or both parties can alert the commissioner to pause the draft to allow extra time to negotiate.

Section 3: Scoring System

3.1 Scoring System

The league scoring system is listed on the Sleeper fantasy app (for the most part it follows typical 0.5 PPR scoring). The scoring system will never change without a majority league vote.

Section 4: Postseason

4.1 Playoffs

At the end of the season 6 teams will make the playoffs. The first round commences in Week 15. Each team that wins their division and the 4 wild card teams with the best records will receive a playoff berth. The 2 division winners receive a bye in the first round. Each playoff matchup lasts one week and the winner advances. Week 18 will not be used.

4.2 Seeding Tiebreakers

If two teams finish with the same record, the tiebreakers go as follows:

  1. Points For
  2. Head-to-Head Record
  3. Division Record
  4. Total Points Against
  5. Coin Flip
  6. Duel to the death

Section 5: Tanking Policy

5.1 League Tanking Policy

No team may intentionally leave starting roster spots empty. Any evidence of intentionally leaving roster spots empty in order to improve your draft position may be subject to punishment, including but not limited to the forfeiture of draft selections.

Section 6: Replacing Managers

6.1 Removing Managers

Short of continuously failing to set a valid lineup, or confirmed collusion, no manager can be removed against their will. Participation through the league chat, trade offers, and the waiver wire is strongly encouraged but is generally not grounds for removal.

6.2 Replacing Managers

When a manager needs to be replaced, the commissioners will try to find a suitable candidate with some connection to at least a portion of the existing managers. Priority will be placed on managers who are eager to take on a dynasty commitment and will be very active within the league.

6.3 Replacement Incentive

In the event that a manager chooses to quit the league or is removed by a commissioner, an incoming manager is offered a 50% reduction on their first year buy-in.

Section 7: League Finances

7.1 League Dues

League dues are set at $200. Dues are collected through LeagueSafe and collection will be sent out no later than August 1st. All managers must be fully paid up before the start of regular season. In the event that a manager fails to pay before the deadline, a 10% penalty will be added to their payment and will go towards the payouts at the end of the year.

7.2 Payout

League payout is structured as follows:

  • 1st place: $1,600
  • 2nd place: $600
  • 3rd place: $200

In the event that a manager was fined due to late payment, their extra fee will be added to the winner's payout. Similarly, if new managers come into the league and the payout is smaller, the difference will come out of the winner's prize.

7.3 Raising Dues

A ⅔ majority vote is required in order to raise league dues. In the event that a manager is no longer financially comfortable with the buy-in, finding a co-manager to split the cost is recommended.
